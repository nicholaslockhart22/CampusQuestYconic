# CampusQuest Prompt Library

This document contains polished prompt templates for developing the CampusQuest concept. These are written for ideation, planning, writing, and design support only. They are not meant to be executed automatically.

## Core Product Brief

Use this as shared context before any prompt:

> CampusQuest is a student-focused mobile app that turns college life into a real-world RPG. Students earn XP for productive activities like studying, working out, attending events, joining clubs, and building professional connections. The app combines gamification, social accountability, streaks, challenges, and community features to help students stay motivated, consistent, and engaged. Core themes include progress, discipline, social connection, and turning real-life growth into something visible and rewarding.

## Prompt 1: Product Vision

Use this prompt when you want a strong high-level articulation of the idea:

```text
Act as a startup strategist and product storyteller.

Using the following concept, write a polished product vision for CampusQuest:

CampusQuest is a student-focused mobile app that turns college life into a real-world RPG. Students earn XP for productive activities such as studying, going to the gym, attending events, networking, and participating in campus life. The platform combines gamification, social interaction, accountability, and progress tracking to help students stay motivated and consistent.

Write the response with these sections:
- Overview
- Problem
- Vision
- Solution
- Why It Matters

Keep the tone clear, modern, ambitious, and investor-ready.
```

## Prompt 10: Long-Term Product Roadmap

Use this when mapping the future direction of the company:

```text
Act as a startup product strategist.

Create a 3-phase roadmap for CampusQuest.

Phase 1 should cover MVP and student adoption.
Phase 2 should cover campus expansion and community features.
Phase 3 should cover partnerships, monetization, and advanced personalization.

For each phase, include:
- Main goal
- Key features
- User impact
- Business impact

Make the roadmap ambitious but realistic.
```

## Writing Guidelines

When using any of these prompts, aim for:

- Clear, direct language
- Strong product positioning
- A modern, ambitious tone
- Practical output over vague hype
- Student-centered messaging with startup-level polish
